About the Experience

Tamale isn't
a stop. It's
the destination.

Voted three times as Ghana's cleanest city, Tamale is the vibrant capital of the Northern Region — a city where ancient Dagomba traditions thrive alongside modern life. For too long, travelers passed through on the way to Mole. We say: stay. Eat. Discover.

TasteTamale is a curated 3-day monthly workshop that immerses you in the flavors, crafts, people, and landscapes of northern Ghana — guided by Salma and Basira, two women who know every corner of this city from the inside out.

What Awaits You

Food. Culture. Magic.

🍲

Tuo Zaafi & Ayoyo

Tamale's soul food — a thick maize-millet dough served with rich leafy green soups, ground dawadawa, and spiced stews. Eaten communally, the way food should be.

🔥

Grilled Guinea Fowl

At the legendary Mba Yahaya's stall — smoky, spiced guinea fowl straight off the grill. The unofficial street food king of Tamale. A must.

🏺

Kukuo Pottery Village

Watch local women shape clay into art with their bare hands — a pottery tradition passed down for generations in this ancient village suburb of Tamale.

🐘

Mole National Park

Just 2 hours away — Ghana's largest wildlife reserve. Elephants, antelopes, baboons and hundreds of bird species in their natural savannah habitat.

🕌

Larabanga Mosque

Built in 1421 — one of the oldest mosques in West Africa. A stunning example of Sudanese mud-and-stick architecture, and a place of profound peace.

🧺

Central Market & Crafts

Woven baskets, leather goods, smocks, brass openers, shea butter — Tamale's beating commercial heart. Locals say you haven't seen Ghana until you've been lost in this market.

🌅

Harmattan Sunset at Giddipas

Climb to the rooftop terrace of Giddipas with a cold Toose or lamujee ginger drink in hand, and watch the sun melt over the savannah skyline.

🥣

Waakye & Street Flavors

The best waakye in Ghana is debated here — rice and beans heaped with spaghetti, fish, beef, and a bold black pepper sauce. For under ₵20. Seriously.

🎭

Dagomba Drumming & Dance

The National Cultural Centre hosts traditional performances — immerse yourself in the rhythmic heartbeat of Dagbon Kingdom culture and its living traditions.

The 3-Day Journey

Every Day Brighter
Than the Last

01

Arrival & Flavour First

Feel the pulse of the city

  • Morning Arrival & welcome at accommodation. Orientation walk through Tamale's old town.
  • Midday Central Market immersion — textile shops, food stalls, shea butter vendors.
  • Afternoon Visit the National Cultural Centre — arts, crafts, history exhibits.
  • Evening Street food feast: grilled guinea fowl at Mba Yahaya's + waakye from the legendary spot opposite VRA.
  • Night Sunset drinks at Giddipas rooftop with Toose & lamujee.
02

Culture, Clay & the Wild

Traditions you won't find in books

  • Dawn Early departure for Mole National Park — morning safari walk, elephants at the waterhole.
  • Midday Larabanga Mosque visit — West Africa's oldest standing mosque (built 1421).
  • Afternoon Kukuo Pottery Village — watch women craft clay by hand & try it yourself.
  • Evening Traditional Tuo Zaafi cooking class with a local family, followed by communal dinner.
  • Night Dagomba drumming performance at the Cultural Centre.
03

Deep North & Farewell Feast

Memories you'll carry forever

  • Morning Visit Sognaayili — a traditional nucleated thatch settlement where farming, life & community are one.
  • Midday Tannery tour and the traditional leather craft market.
  • Afternoon Free time — optional bicycle ride through town, or browse the basket weavers' stalls.
  • Evening Grand farewell feast: full Northern Ghanaian spread — fufu with groundnut soup, kelewele, pito, and more.
  • Night Departure or optional extended stay arranged separately.
